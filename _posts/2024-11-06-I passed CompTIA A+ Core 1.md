Well, I successfully overcame those distractions! After really focusing in on the exam objectives and sidelining everything else, I felt ready to bring it to the exam room. My local exam centre didn't support CompTIA, so I had to install Pearson's 'Onvue'
software and set up a home exam space. It was a stressful prep experience, in that my upstairs neighbours were clomping about like deranged elephants (you can be excluded from the exam if the proctor thinks you're in receipt of morse code), one of my other
neighbours was outside working very audibly on his car, and the onvue software decided it would continually change my desktop background back to an image of common port numbers. I spent an hour troubleshooting that issue, even going into the registry editor
of Windows, to no avail!
<br>
<br>
Eventually, however, I got everything running and was able to sit the exam. Channelling my inner Linus, I answered to the best of my ability, and although a few of the performance based questions didn't fully make sense to me given the context, I passed with
a final score of 720. I was convinced I had failed when some questions popped up that nothing to do with the exam objectives I had studied (I later verified this - this was supposed to happen) and was genuinely overjoyed when the passing grade came in!
<br>
<br>
This week sees me doing the initial groundwork for Core 2, which is primarily watching videos, cross referencing the exam objectives, and googling anything that needs a bit of a deeper look. Overall I am pretty happy, and looking forward to the ups and downs
of the next few weeks!
